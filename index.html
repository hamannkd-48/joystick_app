Statera Manus: Finding Balance

In Latin,

Statera translates to "balance" and "scales." We chose this name because it perfectly embodies our core mission: to restore digital balance and accessibility to individuals with hand tremors, essential tremor, parkinsons, MS, or withdrawals.


We Believe

that technology should empower, not frustrate. By compensating for involuntary movements, the adaptive Statera joystick acts as the scales, leveling the playing field and allowing users to navigate the digital world with confidence, precision, and ease. We believe in the power of design.


Built for Accessibility

Every feature designed to give users with tremors confident, precise computer control.

Adaptive Filtering

1 Euro Filter with median spike removal adapts in real-time to your movement speed -- smooth when idle, responsive when intentional.

Auto-Calibration

Automatic center recalibration detects when the stick is still and corrects drift -- plus manual center and range calibration.

Browser-Based Config

No software install needed. Connect via Web Serial, adjust profiles, remap buttons, and upload -- all in your browser.

Expandable I/O

Add micro switches, buttons, IR sensors, or sip-puff interfaces. Map any input to any function.

Live Testing

Built-in snake game and interactive button map let you verify everything works before you rely on it.

Cloud Save

Sign in with Google to save your configuration. Your profiles, mappings, and calibration persist across sessions.

How It Works

Three steps from unboxing to full mouse control.

1

Wire

Connect the joystick, safety jumper, and any extra buttons to your Arduino Leonardo. The app shows you exactly what goes where.

2

Configure

Choose a tremor profile, remap buttons, add sensors. Upload your config directly from the browser or export the sketch for Arduino IDE.

3

Test

Play the built-in snake game to verify cursor control. Watch the live button map to confirm every input fires correctly.
